FAQ

How long does a typical edit take?
Depends on the length and complexity of the project. Standard short-form edits (30–60 sec) usually take a few days. Heavily edited longer pieces — 90 seconds with full motion graphics — can take 7–13 days. I'll always give you a realistic timeline before starting.
Do you offer revisions?
Yes, revisions are included. The only thing that falls outside of revisions is major structural changes after the edit is finished — like a full music swap. Pacing, cuts, colour, text adjustments are all fair game.
Do I need to provide music?
No. I choose music based on what's currently trending and what fits the content. If you have a specific track in mind, I'm happy to use it — but it's not required.
What format do you need the footage in?
Phone footage can be great — it really comes down to how it's recorded. Good lighting, steady hands or a gimbal, and shooting in the highest resolution your device allows makes a big difference. Low resolution (like 720p) will limit what's possible in post, but I'll always work with what you've got and be upfront about what to expect.
Vertical or horizontal?
Both. Whether you're posting Reels and TikToks in 9:16 or YouTube/landscape content in 16:9, I can work with either format — or both if you need the same edit cut for multiple platforms.
